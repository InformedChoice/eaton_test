**English:** and the boy carried the wooden boat with the coiled, hard-braided brown lines, the gaff and the harpoon with its shaft.  
**Spanish:** El niño llevaba el bote de madera con las cuerdas marrones enrolladas, el garfio y el arpón con su palo.

  
**English:** The box with the baits was under the stern of the skiff along with the club that was used to subdue the big fish when they were brought alongside.  
**Spanish:** La caja con el cebo estaba debajo de la parte de atrás del bote, junto con un palo que se usaba para golpear a los peces grandes cuando los traían al bote.

  
**English:** No one would steal from the old man, but it was better to take the sail and the heavy lines home as the dew was bad for them.  
**Spanish:** Nadie le robaba al viejo, pero era mejor llevarse la vela y las cuerdas gruesas a casa, porque el rocío las dañaba.

  
**English:** Though he was quite sure no local people would steal from him, the old man thought that a gaff and a harpoon were needless temptations to leave in a boat.  
**Spanish:** Aunque estaba seguro de que nadie del pueblo le robaría, el viejo pensaba que dejar el garfio y el arpón en el bote era una tentación innecesaria.

  
**English:** They walked up the road together to the old man’s shack and went in through its open door.  
**Spanish:** Caminaron juntos por la calle hasta la choza del viejo y entraron por la puerta abierta.

  
**English:** The old man leaned the mast with its wrapped sail against the wall and the boy put the box and the other gear beside it.  
**Spanish:** El viejo apoyó el mástil con la vela enrollada contra la pared, y el niño puso la caja y el resto de las cosas a su lado.

  
**English:** The mast was nearly as long as the one room of the shack.  
**Spanish:** El mástil era casi tan largo como la única habitación de la choza.

  
**English:** The shack was made of the tough budshields of the royal palm which are called guano, and in it there was a bed, a table, one chair, and a place on the dirt floor to cook with charcoal.  
**Spanish:** La choza estaba hecha de las hojas fuertes de la palma real, que se llaman guano, y dentro había una cama, una mesa, una silla y un lugar en el suelo de tierra para cocinar con carbón.

  
**English:** On the brown walls of the flattened, overlapping leaves of the sturdy, fibered guano there was a picture in color of the Sacred Heart of Jesus and another of the Virgin of Cobre.  
**Spanish:** En las paredes marrones hechas con hojas de guano, había una foto a color del Sagrado Corazón de Jesús y otra de la Virgen de Cobre.

  
**English:** These were relics of his wife.  
**Spanish:** Estos eran recuerdos de su esposa.

  
**English:** Once there had been a tinted photograph of his wife on the wall, but he had taken it down because it made him too lonely to see it, and it was on the shelf in the corner under his clean shirt.  
**Spanish:** Antes había una foto de su esposa en la pared, pero él la quitó porque lo hacía sentir solo, y la puso en un estante en la esquina, debajo de su camisa limpia.

  
**English:** “What do you have to eat?” the boy asked.  
**Spanish:** —¿Qué vas a comer? —preguntó el niño.

  
**English:** “A pot of yellow rice with fish.”  
**Spanish:** —Un plato de arroz amarillo con pescado.

  
**English:** “Do you want some?”  
**Spanish:** —¿Quieres un poco?

  
**English:** “No.”  
**Spanish:** —No.

  
**English:** “I will eat at home.”  
**Spanish:** —Comeré en casa.

  
**English:** “Do you want me to make the fire?”  
**Spanish:** —¿Quieres que haga el fuego?

  
**English:** “No. I will make it later on.”  
**Spanish:** —No. Lo haré después.

  
**English:** “Or I may eat the rice cold.”  
**Spanish:** —O puedo comer el arroz frío.

  
**English:** “May I take the cast net?”  
**Spanish:** —¿Puedo llevar la red de pesca?

  
**English:** “Of course.”  
**Spanish:** —Claro.

  
**English:** There was no cast net, and the boy remembered when they had sold it.  
**Spanish:** No había red de pesca, y el niño recordó cuando la habían vendido.

  
**English:** But they went through this fiction every day.  
**Spanish:** Pero contaban ese cuento todos los días.

  
**English:** There was no pot of yellow rice with fish, and the boy knew this too.  
**Spanish:** No había arroz amarillo con pescado, y el niño lo sabía.

  
**English:** “Eighty-five is a lucky number,” the old man said.  
**Spanish:** —Ochenta y cinco es un número de suerte —dijo el viejo.

  
**English:** “How would you like to see me bring one in that dressed out over a thousand pounds?”  
**Spanish:** —¿Te gustaría verme traer uno que pese más de mil libras?

  
**English:** “I’ll get the cast net and go for sardines.”  
**Spanish:** —Voy a buscar la red de pesca y a ir por sardinas.

  
**English:** “Will you sit in the sun in the doorway?”  
**Spanish:** —¿Te sentarás al sol en la puerta?

  
**English:** “Yes.”  
**Spanish:** —Sí.

  
**English:** “I have yesterday’s paper and I will read the baseball.”  
**Spanish:** —Tengo el periódico de ayer y voy a leer sobre béisbol.

  
**English:** The boy did not know whether yesterday’s paper was a fiction too.  
**Spanish:** El niño no sabía si el periódico de ayer también era un cuento.

  
**English:** But the old man brought it out from under the bed.  
**Spanish:** Pero el viejo lo sacó de debajo de la cama.

  
**English:** “Perico gave it to me at the bodega,” he explained.  
**Spanish:** —Perico me lo dio en la tienda —explicó él.

  
**English:** “I’ll be back when I have the sardines.”  
**Spanish:** —Volveré cuando tenga las sardinas.

  
**English:** “I’ll keep yours and mine together on ice and we can share them in the morning.”  
**Spanish:** —Guardaré las sardinas en hielo y las compartiremos en la mañana.

  
**English:** “When I come back you can tell me about the baseball.”  
**Spanish:** —Cuando regrese, me contarás sobre el béisbol.

  
**English:** “The Yankees cannot lose.”  
**Spanish:** —Los Yankees no pueden perder.

  
**English:** “But I fear the Indians of Cleveland.”  
**Spanish:** —Pero tengo miedo de los Indians de Cleveland.

**Spanish:** “Ten fe en los Yankees, hijo.”

  
**English:** “Think of the great DiMaggio.”  
**Spanish:** “Recuerda al gran DiMaggio.”

  
**English:** “I fear both the Tigers of Detroit and the Indians of Cleveland.”  
**Spanish:** “Tengo miedo de los Tigres de Detroit y de los Indios de Cleveland.”

  
**English:** “Be careful or you will fear even the Reds of Cincinnati and the White Sax of Chicago.”  
**Spanish:** “Ten cuidado o tendrás miedo de los Rojos de Cincinnati y de los Blancos de Chicago.”

  
**English:** “You study it and tell me when I come back.”  
**Spanish:** “Estúdialo y dime cuando regrese.”

  
**English:** “Do you think we should buy a lottery machine with an eighty-five?  
Tomorrow is the eighty-fifth day.”  
**Spanish:** “¿Crees que debemos comprar una máquina de lotería con el número 85?  
Mañana es el día 85.”

  
**English:** “We can do that,” the boy said. “But what about the eighty-seven of your great record?”  
**Spanish:** “Podemos hacerlo,” dijo el niño. “Pero, ¿y el 87 de tu gran récord?”

  
**English:** “[17] It could not happen twice.”  
**Spanish:** “[17] No puede pasar dos veces.”

  
**English:** “Do you think you can find an eighty-five?”  
**Spanish:** “¿Puedes encontrar un 85?”

  
**English:** “I can order one.”  
**Spanish:** “Puedo pedir uno.”

  
**English:** “One sheet.”  
**Spanish:** “Una hoja.”

  
**English:** “That’s two dollars and a half.”  
**Spanish:** “Son dos dólares y medio.”

  
**English:** “Who can we borrow that from?”  
**Spanish:** “¿De quién se lo podemos pedir?”

  
**English:** “That’s easy.”  
**Spanish:** “Es fácil.”

  
**English:** “I can always borrow two dollars and a half.”  
**Spanish:** “Siempre puedo pedir dos dólares y medio prestados.”

  
**English:** “I think perhaps I can too.”  
**Spanish:** “Creo que yo también puedo.”

  
**English:** “But I try not to borrow.”  
**Spanish:** “Pero trato de no pedir prestado.”

  
**English:** “First you borrow.”  
**Spanish:** “Primero pides prestado.”

  
**English:** “Then you beg.”  
**Spanish:** “Después suplicas.”

  
**English:** “Keep warm, old man,” the boy said.  
**Spanish:** “Abrígate, viejo,” dijo el niño.

  
**English:** “Remember we are in September.”  
**Spanish:** “Recuerda, es septiembre.”

  
**English:** “The month when the great fish come,” the old man said.  
**Spanish:** “El mes en que vienen los peces grandes,” dijo el viejo.

  
**English:** “Anyone can be a fisherman in May.”  
**Spanish:** “Cualquiera puede ser pescador en mayo.”

  
**English:** “I go now for the sardines,” the boy said.  
**Spanish:** “Voy ahora por las sardinas,” dijo el niño.

  
**English:** When the boy came back the old man was asleep in the chair and the sun was down.  
**Spanish:** Cuando el niño regresó, el viejo dormía en la silla y el sol ya se había puesto.

  
**English:** The boy took the old army blanket off the bed and spread it over the back of the chair and over the old man’s shoulders.  
**Spanish:** El niño quitó la manta vieja de la cama y la puso sobre el respaldo de la silla y los hombros del viejo.

  
**English:** They were strange shoulders, still powerful although very old, and the neck was still strong too; the creases did not show so much when the old man was asleep and his head had fallen forward.  
**Spanish:** Sus hombros eran extraños, fuertes pero viejos, y su cuello seguía siendo fuerte; las arrugas se notaban menos cuando el viejo dormía y su cabeza caía hacia adelante.

  
**English:** His shirt had been patched so many times that it was like the sail, and the patches were faded to many different shades by the sun.  
**Spanish:** Su camisa estaba remendada tantas veces que parecía la vela, y los parches se habían aclarado por el sol.

  
**English:** The old man’s head was very old, and with his eyes closed there was no life in his face.  
**Spanish:** La cabeza del viejo era muy vieja, y con los ojos cerrados, su cara parecía sin vida.

  
**English:** The newspaper lay across his knees and the weight of his arm held it there in the evening breeze.  
**Spanish:** El periódico estaba sobre sus rodillas, y su brazo lo sostenía con la brisa de la tarde.

  
**English:** He was barefooted.  
**Spanish:** Él iba descalzo.

  
**English:** The boy left him there, and when he came back the old man was still asleep.  
**Spanish:** El niño lo dejó allí, y cuando regresó, el viejo seguía dormido.

  
**English:** “Wake up, old man,” the boy said and put his hand on one of the old man’s knees.  
**Spanish:** “Despierta, viejo,” dijo el niño y puso su mano en una de las rodillas del viejo.

  
**English:** The old man opened his eyes and for a moment he was coming back from a long way away.  
**Spanish:** El viejo abrió los ojos y por un momento pareció volver de muy lejos.

  
**English:** Then he smiled.  
**Spanish:** Luego, sonrió.

  
**English:** “What have you got?” he asked.  
**Spanish:** “¿Qué tienes?” preguntó él.

  
**English:** “Supper,” said the boy.  
**Spanish:** “La cena,” dijo el niño.

  
**English:** “We’re going to have supper.”  
**Spanish:** “Vamos a cenar.”
